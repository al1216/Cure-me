Plasma Donor


What is Plasma Therapy?

Convalescent plasma therapy uses blood from people who’ve recovered from an illness to help others recover. Blood donated by recovered COVID-19 patients has antibodies to the virus. The donated blood is processed to remove blood cells, leaving behind liquid (plasma) and antibodies. These can be administered to COVID-19 patients to boost their ability to fight the virus.

Why should You Donate Plasma?

Donating plasma once can save up to three lives. Do we really need a better reason than that? This is your time to be a hero, Be One.

Who can Donate Plasma?

Should weigh 50 kilos and above Must be between the ages of 18 and 60. Tested Covid+ in last 120 days Fully recovered from COVID-19 for at least three weeks. Women who have not conceived a child. Not suffering from any transmissible disease.

Who cannot Donate Plasma?

Those weighing less than 50 kilos. Those having diabetics and on insulin. Those with B.P more than 140, and diastolic less than 60 or more than 90. Cancer survivors. Chronic kidney/heart/lung or liver disease. Women who have been pregnant in the past. People who received vaccination in the past 2 months.

Is Plasma Donation Safe?

The process is 100% safe with no major side effects. Occasionally, people experience minor fatigue or dehydration issues. Please ensure that you take full precautions when you visit the centre for plasma donation.
